GROWTH HUB – MEETING SCRIPT (Dan Williams)

A calm, structured run-through for the Teams board meeting.

1. Opening Line

“Good morning, and thank you for your time. I’m Dan Williams from Dursley. I’ve built a set of practical tools over the last few weeks that help with revenue clarity, battery stability, and data handling — all running on low-cost hardware.”

2. What I’m Actually Proposing (The 3 Pillars)

Pillar 1 – Revenue & Data Truth
Tools that give small businesses simple projections, churn modelling, and capacity planning. No jargon — just clean, honest numbers.

Pillar 2 – Battery & Hardware Lab
A small, realistic test space to explore vibration, thermal load and stability features. Goal: +10% battery life improvements through smarter design, not magic.

Pillar 3 – Compression & System Layer
Tools that shrink data while preserving structure — useful for archives, sensors, museums, labs.”

3. Why Only These 3?

“You’ll see five pillars in the full pack — that’s the full long-term horizon. For Phase 1, I’m only asking you to consider these three. The education and heritage pillars come later *once the core is stable, funded, and delivering value*.”

4. What I’m Asking the Board

“I’m looking for guidance on the best structure — sole trader, CIC, or startup — and support connecting with: schools, Uni of Bristol, local museums, and businesses.

I’d also like help shaping a pilot lab — something small, affordable, and deliverable within months.”

5. What Makes This Useful Locally

“Everything I’m building runs on cheap devices — phones, Raspberry Pi, school laptops. It’s a low-cost way to bring coding, physics, and problem-solving back into Dursley, Cam and Berkeley.

My goal is to keep this work rooted locally so the benefits — skills, jobs, inspiration — stay in the district.”

6. Closing Line

“That’s the overview. I’m happy to walk through the demo or answer any questions. Thank you again for the opportunity.”
