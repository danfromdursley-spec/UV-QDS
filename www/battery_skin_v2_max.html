1 ▸ Layer Stack

The Battery Habitat Skin v2+ wraps a cell or module in a thin, intelligent “nano-skin” formed of three functional layers. Together, they expose history-aware colour patterns, high-bandwidth ionic-electronic sensing and a structural interface tuned to QDS Habitat geometries (honeycomb, Severn-flow, QDS-Glass-Carbon).

2 ▸ Signals & Outputs

The skin exposes both analogue signals (sense lines, OECT-style channels, local impedance) and digital observables (colour pattern via camera). Fusing these gives a 2D “health map” for each pack, ready to drop straight into the QDS labs.

  • Sense lines from nano-skin to BMS / logger.
  • Optional fixed camera watching mechanocromic pattern.
  • Feeds real-time 2D stress map into QDS Battery Lab dashboards.
QDS Kernel Role

Skin parameters (colour thresholds, sensitivity, layout) are tuned so observed stress & signal correlations match the QDS kernel:

Kernel K(t,r) = σ² exp(−|t| / τ_c) · exp(−|r|² / 2λ_c²)

This allows τ_c, λ_c and lifetime models to be refined from real packs instead of theory alone, closing the loop between substrate physics and engineering practice.

Integration Hooks
  • Streams directly into QDS Battery Lab (stress & life).
  • Side-feeds to Variance / Rotation / Micro Labs for cross-checks.
  • Data-ready for machine-learning or physics-informed QDS models.
Goal: packs that can report how they feel, show where they hurt and teach the model how to age them more gently.
Integration · Habitat

3 ▸ Habitat Interface

The skin is designed to laminate onto QDS Habitat geometries (honeycomb, Severn-flow, QDS-Glass-Carbon) without blocking cooling, access or serviceability.

  • Assembly in dry state; activation via controlled wetting / gel.
  • Compatible with cell-to-cell bridges and carbon-glass carrier frames.
  • Stress-skin tuned to work with 3D-printed “nano-Severn” pore layouts.
Honeycomb & Severn-flow QDS-Glass-Carbon CNC / 3D-print friendly
Engineering note: geometry is chosen so mechanical stiffness, heat paths and ionic routes all follow the same correlation shapes – fewer hot-spots, fewer surprise failures.
