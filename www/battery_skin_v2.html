Concept — Three-Layer Battery Skin

The Battery Habitat Skin v2+ wraps a cell or module in a thin, intelligent “nano-skin” that can show stress visually, sense local behaviour, and shape current & heat flow to match the QDS kernel.

Layer Stack

Top: Mechanochromic Layer
Colour-changing film that records strain / pressure history: green → nominal, amber → elevated, magenta → over-stressed.

Middle: THIN-style Nano-Skin
Conformal ionic–electronic membrane (inspired by bio THIN devices) that couples local potential, impedance and micro-strain into readable signals.

Base: QDS Habitat Interface
Structurally stiff, correlation-shaped interface (honeycomb, Severn flow, QDS-Glass-Carbon) that manages heat, vibration and current distribution.

Primary Functions

• Visual “ECG” map of pack stress via colour pattern.
• Embedded sensing of local voltage / strain / temperature.
• Field shaping to smooth current density and thermal gradients.

ECG-for-cells Abuse history indicator Lifetime diagnostics Habitat integration

Signals & Outputs

The skin exposes both analogue signals (sense lines, OECT-style channels) and digital observables (colour pattern via camera). Fusing these gives a 2D “health map” to fit into the QDS kernel: K(t,r) = σ² exp(-|t|/τ_c) exp(-|r|² / 2λ_c²).

Integration

Habitat

Designed to laminate onto QDS Habitat geometries (honeycomb, Severn-flow, QDS-Glass-Carbon) without blocking cooling or access. Assembly in dry state; activation via controlled wetting / gel.

QDS Kernel Role

K(t,r)

Skin parameters (colour thresholds, sensitivity, layout) tuned so observed stress & signal correlations match the QDS kernel, refining τc, λc, and lifetime models from real packs instead of theory alone.
