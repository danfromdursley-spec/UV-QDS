GrowthHub Jargon Cheat Sheet

Board-safe translations for the Growth Hub meeting. Read top-to-bottom once, then skim before you go in.

Pipeline & Deals

How people move from first contact to money.

Lead Someone who has shown real interest – e.g. a warm intro, referral, or inbound enquiry. In the room: “I’m assuming around six good leads a month – people who actually want to talk, not cold spam.”
Qualified lead (q) A lead that is a good fit and has a real problem and some budget. In the room: “I’m assuming about 35% of leads are properly qualified – that’s the q = 0.35 in the model.”
Sprint Short, fixed-price project to prove value quickly. In the room: “A sprint is a time-boxed test project around £2.5k to prove impact fast.”
Pilot Larger, deeper engagement to test the system in their real environment. In the room: “A pilot is a deeper trial, around £6k, where we run the system on their real data.”
Retainer Ongoing monthly relationship where you support, tune, and extend the system. In the room: “A retainer is ongoing monthly support around £1.5k for tweaks, reporting and new experiments.”

Money & Time

How the cash is counted.

MRR – Monthly Recurring Revenue Money that repeats every month (mainly retainers), not one-off projects. In the room: “MRR is the reliable base – what I can reasonably expect to come in each month from ongoing work.”
Run-rate (revenue / year) “If we froze today’s performance and extended it out for 12 months, what yearly revenue does that imply?” In the room: “The £44k run-rate is just today’s numbers rolled out over a year – it’s a grounded target, not hype.”
Churn (c) Percentage of monthly recurring revenue that cancels each month. In the room: “I’ve assumed 3% monthly churn – some clients will naturally roll off or pause.”
Cost base / month Rough fixed monthly costs to keep the lights on (software, basic salary, etc.). In the room: “I’ve set a simple £200/month cost base here – that grows as we add people, but this keeps the model honest.”

Model Logic

How the calculator actually thinks.

Capacity (Smax, Pmax) How many sprints and pilots you can realistically deliver per month without burning out. In the room: “I’ve capped this at 6 sprints and 3 pilots a month so the model doesn’t assume superhuman hours.”
Conversion rate (q, s, p, r) The percentages at each step of the pipeline: lead → qualified → sprint → pilot → retainer. In the room: “I’ve used modest 0.35 / 0.35 conversion rates – realistic numbers, not ‘salesman-on-coke’ fantasy.”
Concept
Revenue floor A careful, capacity-truthed estimate of what you can earn if things go “normally”, not perfectly. In the room: “This isn’t a moonshot; it’s a revenue floor – what I expect to hit if I simply do the work and keep the pipeline moving.”
Concept
Horizon (12 months) How far the model looks ahead when it accumulates MRR. In the room: “I’ve used a 12-month horizon – enough time to see retainers build without pretending I can predict five years out.”
Concept
Assumption audit A list of the key assumptions with a confidence level beside each one. In the room: “The assumption audit is where we kick the tyres together – I want you to see exactly what I’m assuming and help tighten it up.”
Concept
Sensitivity – “What moves the needle” Shows which small changes (extra lead, better close rate, etc.) have the biggest effect on yearly revenue. In the room: “This table shows where to push first – for example, +1 lead a month adds about £7k/year on this setup.”

One-liner you can quote

“I’ve built this as a revenue floor model – modest conversion rates, realistic capacity, and a 12-month horizon. It’s designed for honest planning, not hype.”
